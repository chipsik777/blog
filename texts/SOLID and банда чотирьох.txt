SOLID and "банда чотирьох"

SOLID and "банда чотирьох" (Gang of Four) are two distinct concepts in software development,
 often mentioned together due to their importance in object-oriented design and programming.

"Банда чотирьох" (Gang of Four or GoF) refers to the four authors - Erich Gamma, Richard Helm, 
Ralph Johnson, and John Vlissides - who wrote the influential book "Design Patterns: Elements of 
Reusable Object-Oriented Software" in 1994[1][4]. This book introduced 23 design patterns that address
 common problems in object-oriented software design[1].

SOLID, on the other hand, is an acronym representing five principles of object-oriented programming and design:

1. Single Responsibility Principle
2. Open-Closed Principle
3. Liskov Substitution Principle
4. Interface Segregation Principle
5. Dependency Inversion Principle

These principles aim to make software designs more understandable, flexible, and maintainable[3][4]. While not directly related to the Gang of Four patterns, SOLID principles are often used in conjunction with design patterns to create high-quality, structured code[3][4].
